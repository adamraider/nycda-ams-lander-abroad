Web Development Intensive

12 Week - Full Time

HTML5, CSS3, FTP, JavaScript, jQuery, Ruby, Sinatra and Rails, Database Theory, ActiveRecord, Command Line, Git, Collaborative Dev

What you'll need

Students will need a 90-day visa to account for the 12-week course. They will also need to book their own flights to and from Amsterdam. Students are expected to find a suitable housing option for the duration of their stay. NYCDA will provide a list of possible housing options upon request. Students must have up-to-date vaccinations before leaving for Amsterdam.

A Mac or PC laptop. We'll help you get it set up with all of the software required.

The motivation and drive to complete an intense, full-time curriculum.

Financing

Courses at The New York Code + Design Academy are primarily financed in one of two ways:

Students may set up a payment plan with NYCDA without taking out a loan or paying interest. Tuition is divided into installments, with the balance due before the end of class.

Students may apply for a loan with one of our financing partners. ClimbCredit, Affirm, Pave, and Skills Fund can offer students a customized payment plan in monthly installments (This option is only available to U.S. students).

Other financing partners and plans are available. If you have any questions, please contact info@nycda.com.

We believe that everyone should have the opportunity to learn to code, and NYCDA offers a variety of finance options for prospective students.

Transportation

There are two main modes of widely used transportation in Amsterdam: public transportation and cycling. Both offer their own distinct advantages and both are simple and easy to use.

Public Transportation

Amsterdam utilizes the public transport chip card (OV-chipkaart) for travel on trams, buses, and metros. GVB day passes provide unlimited travel on the GVB network in Amsterdam (GVB buses, metros, and trams) for a set period of time (1-7 days). Seven day passes will provide the most use and are priced at 32 Euros.

Cycling

Biking provides a healthy, practical, and enjoyable mode of transportation. Amsterdam is extremely bike-friendly with a network of cycle routes and flat landscape. There are bike rentals all over, and for the purpose of safety, many establishments offer brightly-colored bikes to alert pedestrians.

Weather

Weather in Amsterdam is cool and brisk throughout the year. August is the hottest month in Amsterdam, with an average temperature of 17°C (63°F), and the coldest is January, at 3°C (37°F), with the most daily sunshine hours at 11 in June. The wettest month is November, with an average of 90mm of rain. Expect average temperatures of 10°C (50°F) throughout your stay.

Safety

Amsterdam is one of the top 25 safest cities in the world, with a much lower crime rate than many other popular European capitals. In large parts of the city, there are always at least a few people out and about, no matter whether it's Saturday night or Wednesday 3am in the morning.

Students should be advised of dangers such as:

  • Pickpockets
  • Bag/Phone snatchers
  • Getting hit by bikes
  • Getting hit by trams
  • Drugs and alcohol
